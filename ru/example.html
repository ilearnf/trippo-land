Trippo real-life use case

One day we helped a family of two to get to Gran-Kanaria from Vilnius. Usuall price of direct flight (by direct we mean single ticket flight) is about 250$ - so that the total price of 2 return tickets would be about $1000 - seems to be a lot, right?

This family did not care about slightly difficult transportation options but asked us to make stops for a day minimum. During this day they could explore the city - so in their trip they would visit several cities and countries and get more impressions because of that.

We offered them 3 different options:

1. 45 eur

Take WizzAir flight to Dormund (15 eur), then FlixBus ($7, 2h) to Cologne, make a stop there for a day, and finally take RyanAir flight from there to Gran-Kanaria (25 eur). So the ticket price would be 45 eur. This option is slightly difficult but it is cheap, and you can explore another city also.

2. 55 eur

We can replace the bus in the first option with a train. It would be 55 eur and a hour less.

3. 75 eur

And the most comfortable option - take a WizzAir to Barcelona (35 eur), make a little stop there and then use RyanAir flight from Barcelona to Gran-Kanaria (40 eur). In this option they would visit Barcelona for 6-7 hours and it is pretty comfortable. But the total price is 75 eur.

You can see that we offered them different kinds of options - for the money and for the comfort. But every option costs much less than direct flight. So the economy is about 750 eur, isn't it a lot? You can make another trip for these money.
